Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Riding a bike safely is essential. Always follow road safety rules—this includes observing traffic signs and signals, wearing a helmet, and being aware of your surroundings. Keep your bike well-maintained and use lights or reflective gear if riding at night to stay visible.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Helmets should generally be replaced every 3-5 years, as materials can degrade over time. If your helmet has visible cracks, worn straps, or has been in an accident, it’s time to get a new one. Always look for certification labels to ensure your helmet meets current safety standards.

My bike has been in storage; is it safe to ride?

Before riding, inspect your bike thoroughly. Check the tires for air and any signs of cracks, test the brakes, and ensure the chain is well-lubricated and free of rust. If the bike has been stored for a long time, consider taking it to a professional for a full tune-up.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Follow local traffic laws just as you would in a car. Ride with the flow of traffic, signal your turns, and stay in designated bike lanes when available. Avoid using headphones while riding, as staying alert to sounds around you is essential for safety.
